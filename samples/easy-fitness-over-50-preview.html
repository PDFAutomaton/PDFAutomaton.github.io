Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before we dive into the full 6-week system, let's establish your baseline. You need to know where you're starting so you can measure where you're going. This isn't about being "good" or "bad"—it's about having a clear before picture. Once you do this, you'll have your first concrete benchmark.

1
Test your chair sit-to-stand (this measures leg strength and mobility)

Sit in a sturdy chair with armrests. Without using your hands, stand up and sit back down slowly and controlled. Count how many full repetitions you can do in 30 seconds with good form (full extension when standing, controlled lowering when sitting). Write this number down. This is your Week 0 baseline. Most inactive women over 60 perform 8–14 repetitions; if you do fewer, that's okay—that's exactly why we're doing this program.

2
Test your grip strength (this measures upper body and functional capacity)

If you have a basic hand grip dynamometer (about $15 online), squeeze it with your dominant hand at full effort for 3 seconds. If you don't have one, that's fine—you can use this alternative: hold a standard can of soup or water bottle at arm's length from your body for as long as you can (time in seconds). Record the number or time. This tells you if your upper body has the capacity to lift, carry, and hold things safely. This matters more than you think for daily independence.

3
Test your balance and reach (this measures fall risk and mobility)

Stand barefoot with your feet hip-width apart. Reach both arms forward as far as you can while keeping your heels on the ground and your body stable. Have someone mark or measure how far your fingertips reach, or note if you felt wobbly or had to step forward. Next, stand on one leg (hold onto a counter) and time how long you can stay balanced. Do this for each leg. If you can't hold 20 seconds, this is a priority area. Record the time for each leg.

After this: You now have three concrete baseline measurements. Keep these written down—you'll retest them at Week 3 and Week 6. In 6 weeks, most women who follow this system see a 20–40% improvement in these numbers. That's measurable. That's real. And that's how you'll know this is working.
