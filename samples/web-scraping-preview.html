1
Install Web Scraper and open your first target site

Open Google Chrome and go to the Chrome Web Store. Search for 'Web Scraper - Free Web Scraping' and install the extension by Web Scraper (it's free and has over 500,000 users). Once installed, navigate to any Amazon product category page relevant to your market — for example, search Amazon for your main product category and land on the results page. This is the page you're going to scrape. Keep that tab open.

2
Create a sitemap and select the data you want to extract

Press F12 to open Chrome DevTools, then click the 'Web Scraper' tab that now appears at the top of the panel. Click 'Create new sitemap,' then 'Create sitemap.' Give it any name (like 'amazon-test') and paste in the URL of the Amazon results page you have open. Hit 'Create sitemap.' Now click 'Add new selector,' select type 'Text,' click 'Select' and then physically click on the first product title on the page — Web Scraper will highlight it in green. Click a second product title and it will expand the selection to capture all matching items on the page. Check the 'Multiple' checkbox, name it 'product-title,' and save the selector. Repeat this process once more for the price field on the same page.

3
Run the scrape and export your data to a spreadsheet

In the Web Scraper panel, click 'Scrape' on your sitemap, then hit 'Start scraping.' A new browser window will open, do its work automatically, and close. Back in the panel, click 'Export data as CSV.' Open that CSV file in Google Sheets or Excel. You now have a live, structured list of product names and prices pulled directly from Amazon — data that would have taken 45 minutes to copy and paste manually, done in under two minutes of actual work.
