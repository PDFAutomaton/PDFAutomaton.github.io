Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before building infrastructure, you need proof that scraping works for your specific data source. This 30-minute exercise validates your approach and gives you your first extracted dataset.

1
Identify and inspect your target

Pick one page containing the data you need. Open it in your browser. Right-click → Inspect. Look for the HTML structure holding your data. Note the CSS selectors or XPath patterns. This takes 5 minutes and confirms the data is accessible.

2
Write a minimal extraction script

Use requests + BeautifulSoup (Python) or similar. Write 8-12 lines of code that fetch the page and extract one data field. Don't optimize. Don't scale. Just extract. This takes 15 minutes and proves the mechanics work.

3
Run it and validate output

Execute the script. Save the output to a CSV or JSON file. Open it. Verify the data matches what you see on the page. This takes 5 minutes and confirms your approach is sound.

After this: You'll have 20-100 rows of clean extracted data in a structured format. More importantly, you'll know whether your target requires JavaScript rendering, authentication, or rate limiting—critical information for building a real system.
