Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you move through the full system, run through this abbreviated version. It isolates the three non-negotiable steps that make the difference between a set that holds and one that fails. You'll see immediately why technique matters more than timing.

1
Prepare the nail surface with intention

File your natural nails short (under 2mm) and create texture on the nail bed using a buffer or nail file. This isn't aesthetic—it's mechanical. The texture gives adhesive something to grip. Wipe away all dust with a slightly damp cloth and let dry completely. A smooth, clean surface is where 60% of adhesion happens.

2
Size and apply without air pockets

Match each press-on nail to your natural nail—it should cover edge-to-edge with no gaps. Apply a thin, even layer of adhesive (or use adhesive tabs, pressed firmly). Press straight down for 15 seconds with slight downward pressure. Roll your finger gently side-to-side to eliminate air bubbles. Air pockets break the seal and cause lifting.

3
Secure the seam and cure

Once all nails are applied, press down firmly around the edges and seams for another 10 seconds per nail. If using nail glue, wait 5 minutes before touching. If using tabs, the set is ready to wear. This final pressure creates a continuous seal that determines longevity.

After this: A full set of nails that feels solid, looks seamless, and will hold for at least two weeks with normal wear. No lifting at the edges. No separation at the seams. You'll understand exactly why this happened, which means you can repeat it.
