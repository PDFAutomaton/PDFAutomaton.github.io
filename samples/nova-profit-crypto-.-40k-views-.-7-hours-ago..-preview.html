Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read another chapter, do this one exercise right now. Pick any coin you are currently holding or seriously considering buying — it could be something you saw on the Nova Profit channel, a meme coin someone sent you on WhatsApp, or even something you already lost money on. We are going to run it through a 3-step Legitimacy Check that takes 30 minutes and will immediately show you whether your current thinking about that coin is based on signal or noise. Start with Bismillah and a clear niyyah — you are not gambling here, you are learning to make decisions with ilm, with knowledge, which is an obligation our deen places on all of us.

1
Find the Withdrawal Proof — Not the Promise

Go to YouTube or Telegram and search the coin name plus the word 'withdrawal proof' or 'payment proof.' Do not look at the project's own channel. Look for independent users showing actual bank transfers or wallet screenshots with timestamps. This is exactly what was missing from Treasure NFT and Magic NFT before they collapsed — there were endless update videos but almost zero verified third-party withdrawal proof. If you cannot find at least 3 independent withdrawal proofs from people who have no obvious reason to promote the project, write the word UNVERIFIED next to the coin name on a piece of paper. This step alone would have saved thousands of people from Treasure Fun.

2
Check the Liquidity Lock and Contract on DEXTools or Token Sniffer

Open DEXTools.io or TokenSniffer.com and paste the coin's contract address. You are looking for three things: Is the liquidity locked for at least 6 months? Is the contract ownership renounced or does one wallet control everything? And what percentage of the supply does the top 10 wallets hold? If liquidity is not locked, the developers can pull it out overnight — this is called a rug pull and it is haram in every sense, financial and ethical. If one wallet holds more than 20 percent of the supply, write HIGH RISK next to the coin name. Little Pepe and coins like it get hyped as the next PEPE but most fail this check within 30 seconds.

3
Set Your Exit Before You Enter — Write It on Paper Right Now

This is the step almost no one in this audience does and it is the reason most people either hold too long and lose everything or panic-sell at a 15 percent dip and miss the actual gain. On that same piece of paper write three numbers next to the coin: your entry price, your stop-loss price which should be no more than 10 to 15 percent below entry, and your take-profit target which should be at minimum 2x your risk. For example if you buy at 100 rupees and your stop-loss is 87 rupees your loss is 13 rupees. Your take-profit should be at minimum 126 rupees to make the trade worth taking. If you cannot write these three numbers down before buying, you are not trading — you are gambling. Use your next Salah time as a reset point. If you still feel the same about the trade after Asr that you did before Zuhr, the conviction is real. If the FOMO fades, that is your answer.

After this: After 30 minutes you will have one coin evaluated honestly against real criteria — not hype, not a YouTuber's energy, not a Telegram group's excitement. You will have a piece of paper with either UNVERIFIED, HIGH RISK, or the three numbers that make the trade mechanical instead of emotional. That piece of paper is the beginning of your system. Every profitable trader has a version of it. Now you do too.
