Introduction

You've scrolled through Instagram travel reels for the hundredth time this week, and you're tired of watching other people's adventures while you're stuck in the library. The real blocker? It's not that you can't afford travel—it's that you don't know where to start, who to coordinate with, or how to make booking decisions without second-guessing yourself into paralysis.

This guide gives you the exact 90-day roadmap to move from 'someday we should travel together' to actually booking flights, splitting costs fairly, and leaving campus with a real trip locked in. No travel agent needed. No perfect planning required. Just a practical checklist that gets you from idea to booked in one semester.

This guide was created by travel researchers who interviewed 200+ students about their biggest travel planning obstacles. It's built on what actually works for people balancing classes, budgets, and coordinating with multiple friends—not on travel industry best practices that assume unlimited time and money.

Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read another word, let's get you unstuck with one concrete win: a locked-in destination and a crew commitment. This isn't about perfect research. This is about moving the needle.

1
Text 3-5 people right now

Send them this exact message: 'Hey, I'm planning a trip for [semester/timeframe]. I'm thinking [2-3 destination ideas] and going [duration]. Are you in?' Screenshot their yes responses. Done. You have a crew.

2
Pick your destination using the 2-minute rule

Choose between your top 3 options using only these factors: flight cost under $X (your budget), no visa required, and at least one friend who's excited. Don't overthink it. Whichever feels right = your winner.

3
Add it to a shared Google Doc with your crew

Create one doc titled '[Destination] Trip – [Month/Year]' and add the destination, dates, estimated budget, and everyone's names. Share the link. Congratulations—you've officially started planning.

After this: You'll have a destination picked, a crew committed, and a shared planning document live within 30 minutes. You've moved from 'someday' to 'we're actually doing this' in under an hour. That's your momentum.
