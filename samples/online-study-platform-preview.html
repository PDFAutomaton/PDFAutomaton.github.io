Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you commit to the full system, run this diagnostic to see exactly where your revision is leaking marks. This isn't motivation—it's data. You'll identify one topic area where 2 hours of focused work this week could net you 5–8 marks on your next practice test.

1
Map your syllabus by exam weight

Open your past 3 exam papers (or mock papers). For each topic, count how many marks it carried. Create a simple list: Topic Name | Marks | % of Total. Do this in 10 minutes. You'll likely find that 40% of your marks come from 20% of the syllabus—and you're probably spending equal time on everything.

2
Run a recall test on your top 3 topics

Take the 3 topics that carry the most exam marks. Close all notes. Write down everything you can recall about each in 2–3 minutes per topic. Don't reread anything first—just write what's in your head right now. Be honest about what you actually retain versus what you think you know.

3
Check your recall against the mark scheme

Now open your notes or textbook. For each topic, compare what you recalled to what the exam actually requires. Count how many key points you missed. This gap—between what you think you know and what you actually recall under time pressure—is where your next 5–8 marks live.

After this: You'll have a ranked list of high-weight topics + your actual recall gaps. Pick the one topic where your recall was weakest but the exam weight is highest. That's your focus for this week. Studying strategically beats studying hard.
