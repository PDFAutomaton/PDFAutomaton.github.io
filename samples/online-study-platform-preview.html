Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before diving into the full system, test-run one core principle right now. Pick one recent Guru Sir lecture (any subject). This 30-minute exercise will show you exactly where your current process breaks down—and how the system fixes it. You'll see immediate clarity on what to do differently.

1
Rewatch 10 minutes of your last lecture

Pick any 10-minute segment from a recent Guru Sir lecture. Watch it once, actively. Don't rewind yet. As you watch, pause every 2-3 minutes and write down the ONE core idea in that section—just one sentence. This forces your brain to extract, not just consume.

2
Close everything and recall what you learned

Without looking at your notes or the screen, write down everything you remember from those 10 minutes. Don't worry about perfection—just let it flow. This is your baseline. Now compare it to what Guru Sir actually taught. Notice the gaps. These gaps are exactly what the system fixes.

3
Create one quick practice question from your notes

Look at your one-sentence summaries. Write one exam-style question (multiple choice, numerical, or short answer—whatever your exam format is) that tests one concept. Don't answer it yet. Just write the question. This is what deliberate practice looks like.

After this: You'll see the difference between passive watching and active processing. You'll realize that understanding a lecture and being able to recall/apply it under exam pressure are two different skills. Most importantly, you'll have your first piece of exam-ready content—one question—in 30 minutes. The full system multiplies this across all your subjects and all your lectures, turning months of study into consistent, measurable progress.
