Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read further, complete this exercise. It takes 30 minutes and shows you immediately whether your current study approach has gaps. You'll walk away with a clear map of what needs to change—and proof that the system works.

1
Choose one topic you studied recently

Pick something you've already covered in your exam syllabus—ideally something you studied within the last week. Open a blank document or grab paper. Set a timer for 5 minutes and write everything you remember about this topic without looking at your notes. Don't filter yourself; just write what comes to mind. This reveals what's actually stuck in long-term memory versus what you only recognized when reading.

2
Compare your recall to your original notes

Now look at your original study material for that topic. Highlight what you forgot or got wrong. Count the gaps. Most students find they've retained only 40-50% of what they studied, even for recently covered material. This gap is what causes exam-day blanks. Notice the pattern in what you forgot—was it definitions, examples, connections between ideas, or the sequence of steps?

3
Identify why the gaps exist

Ask yourself: Did I study this topic once and move on? Did I understand it when I read it, but never practice retrieving it from memory? Did I study it without connecting it to related topics or real examples? These three reasons account for 90% of recall failure. Your answer here tells you exactly which part of the Exam-Ready System will transform your results.

After this: You now have concrete evidence of where your current system fails. More importantly, you understand that the problem isn't your memory or intelligence—it's your approach to studying. This clarity is where consistent exam performance begins. Students who complete this exercise and implement the system that follows report recall rates of 75-85% on previously forgotten material within two study cycles.
