Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you invest time in the full system, test it on one recent lecture. You'll see immediate proof that this method works. This single session will show you how much you can actually retain when you use the right approach—and how different it feels from passive review.

1
Choose one lecture you watched in the last 3 days

Pick something you thought you understood but couldn't fully recall later. Close all other tabs. Set a clear intention—you're testing whether active recall actually works. Take 2 minutes to center yourself with Bismillah before starting; intention (niyyah) shapes the quality of your work.

2
Recall without looking at notes: write down everything you remember

Set a timer for 8 minutes. Write freely—concepts, examples, connections. Don't worry about order or perfection. This isn't about being right; it's about pulling information from your memory. You'll be shocked at how much surfaces when you force recall instead of passively reviewing. This is barakah in focused, intentional effort versus scattered re-reading.

3
Check your recall against the lecture. Note the gaps. Understand why you missed them.

Spend 10 minutes comparing what you wrote to what was actually taught. Don't re-memorize—just identify the pattern of what stuck and what didn't. Write one sentence about why specific information didn't stick. This diagnosis is more valuable than the recall itself.

After this: You'll have a clear, evidence-based answer: how much can you actually retain right now, and where the leaks are. More importantly, you'll feel the difference between passive review and active recall. This 30-minute test proves the system works before you commit to the full process.
