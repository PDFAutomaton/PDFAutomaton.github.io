Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before diving into the full system, you'll audit your current study approach and identify your single biggest leak. This isn't theory—it's a diagnostic that shows you exactly where you're losing retention and marks. Many students shift one thing after this exercise and immediately see better recall within a week.

1
Map Your Current Week

Write down how you spent study time this week: hours on each subject, topics covered, time spent on practice vs. revision vs. theory. Be honest. Most students discover they're spending 60% on low-yield topics and only 10% on consolidation.

2
Test Your Retention

Pick any topic you studied 3-5 days ago. Close your notes and write everything you remember about it in 5 minutes. Compare with your original notes. The gap is your leak. This single gap—multiplied across your entire syllabus—is why you're forgetting.

3
Identify Your Leak Type

Ask yourself: Did I forget because I studied once and never revisited? Did I understand surface-level but not the application? Did I study scattered across the week instead of blocked sessions? Most students have one primary leak. Find yours. That's your starting point.

After this: You'll see exactly why your current approach isn't sticking. More importantly, you'll have one clear thing to fix first—not ten vague improvements. Students who do this exercise typically improve recall by 25-40% within two weeks just by addressing that one leak.
