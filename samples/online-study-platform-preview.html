Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read further, run this quick test on one topic you've already studied. This will show you immediately whether your current study method is creating real retention or just the illusion of learning. You'll see within 30 minutes what needs to change.

1
Choose one concept you studied 3-5 days ago

Pick something you felt confident about at the time. Doesn't matter if it's formulas, definitions, or a problem-solving approach. Write down the topic name.

2
Close your notes and attempt to teach it to someone (or to yourself, recorded)

Spend 5 minutes explaining this concept without looking at any reference material. Be specific. Include examples. Notice where you get stuck or blank. This is real retention, not recognition.

3
Now solve 2-3 problems on this topic from a different source than your original practice

Don't use memorized solutions. Apply the concept fresh. Time yourself. Record: Did you solve it confidently? Did you need to reference your notes? How much time did it take compared to exam speed?

After this: You'll see one of three things: (1) Strong retention and speed—your method is working, but it's missing structure for exam consistency. (2) You remember the concept but solve problems slowly—there's a gap between understanding and application speed. (3) You've forgotten more than you expected—your current study method isn't creating durable retention. Whichever you find, this guide directly addresses it with the fix.
