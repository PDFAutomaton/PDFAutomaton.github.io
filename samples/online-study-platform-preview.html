Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read a single chapter of this guide, do this one exercise right now. It takes 30 minutes, requires only your last 3 mock test scores and a pen, and it will immediately tell you which tier of SSC CGL posts you should be targeting versus which ones you are currently over-investing preparation time in. 1
Pull your last 3 full mock test scores and calculate your average normalized score

Open your mock test app or notebook and write down your last 3 Tier-1 full mock scores. Add them and divide by 3. This is your Current Performance Baseline or CPB. If your scores are 141, 137 and 144, your CPB is 140.7. Write this number at the top of a page. If you have Tier-2 mock data as well, note your Paper-1 Maths and Paper-2 English scores separately. Do not skip this step or estimate — the entire matching exercise depends on an honest number, not your best day score or your worst day score.

2
Map your CPB against the Post Tier Chart on page 23 of this guide and mark your Realistic Zone and Stretch Zone

Turn to the Post Tier Chart on page 23. You will see four score bands for General, OBC, SC and ST categories, each mapped to specific posts. Find your category column and locate where your CPB sits. Circle every post listed in your Realistic Zone — these are posts where your current score is within 6 marks of the historical average cutoff. Put a triangle next to every post in your Stretch Zone — these are posts where the gap is 7 to 14 marks, closeable with focused preparation. Draw a line through every post in the Out of Range column. This is not defeat — this is 4 months of wasted energy you just saved yourself.

3
Write your Top 3 Target Posts on a sticky note and photograph it — this becomes your form-filling anchor

From your Realistic and Stretch Zones, pick the 3 posts that match both your score range and your preference for city posting, department type or promotion trajectory. Write them as Post 1, Post 2 and Post 3 on a sticky note. Post 1 should be your most realistic target — highest vacancy count, cutoff closest to your CPB. Post 2 is your primary stretch. Post 3 is your secondary stretch. Take a photo of this note and set it as your phone wallpaper or save it in your study folder. Every preparation decision you make from tomorrow — which topics to prioritize, how many mocks to take, which optional papers to strengthen — should be filtered through whether it moves you closer to these 3 posts specifically.

The panic of seeing 50-plus post options will be replaced by a focused target list built on real cutoff data, not guesswork.

Stop Studying Blind: How to Read Your Score Like a Post Selection Map

Transform your current mock test scores from random numbers into a precise post-targeting compass before you waste another month studying without a destination.

Most CGL aspirants from tier-2/3 cities make the same fatal mistake — they study for 'the exam' instead of studying for a specific post like Income Tax Inspector or Assistant Audit Officer, which have dramatically different cutoff gaps (sometimes 8-12 marks apart in the same tier). Right now, pull up the last 3 SSC CGL final answer key PDFs from 2022, 2023, and 2024 on the SSC official website and write down the post-wise cutoff scores for your category (OBC/General/SC/ST) in Tier-1 and combined scores — this single exercise will show you that AAO requires roughly 160+ combined while Statistical Investigator Grade-II can be secured around 145-150, meaning the same preparation gap separates a gazetted post from a non-gazetted one. Your current mock test average is not your enemy — it is raw data, and once you map it against 3 years of actual cutoffs, you will immediately see whether you are 5 marks away from CSS or 20 marks away from IT Inspector, which completely changes how aggressively you need to target Quant versus English in the next 90 days. This channel covers tier-wise strategy, subject-wise scoring patterns, and post preference order — use that content as fuel only after you have this personal score-to-post gap calculation done, because without it you are essentially memorizing maps for a city you have not decided to visit yet.

  • Open ssc.nic.in right now and download the Final Result PDFs for CGL 2022 and 2023 — locate the post-wise category cutoff table and screenshot it to your phone gallery so it is always accessible during your prep
  • Create a simple 3-column notebook page: Column 1 = Post Name, Column 2 = Your Category Cutoff (last 2 years average), Column 3 = Your Current Mock Average — fill this for at least 8 posts today to visually see your gap
  • Calculate your 'gap score' for your top 3 desired posts and write the number on a sticky note on your study wall — this number (not a vague dream) becomes your daily target from tomorrow
💡 Pro tip: The biggest rank jumpers do not aim for the highest post — they identify the post that sits exactly 7-10 marks above their current average and lock in on that, because closing a 10-mark gap with focused subject strategy is 3x more achievable in 6 months than chasing a 25-mark gap, and once you crack that post, the higher one becomes next year's goal with insider knowledge.
