Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before diving into the full system, you'll experience one core principle immediately. This exercise reveals which topics you actually need to prioritize and exposes knowledge gaps that are costing you marks. Most students discover they've been wasting time on 2-3 topics they don't need while ignoring critical foundational concepts.

1
Map your exam's topic breakdown

Open your exam syllabus and list all major topics. Next to each, write the weightage (percentage of marks). If weightage isn't published, research past papers and note how many questions came from each topic over the last 3-5 exams. This takes 10 minutes and immediately shows you where exam makers focus.

2
Identify your current knowledge level per topic

For each topic, honestly rate yourself: Can I solve past exam questions on this without help? Rate 1 (no idea) to 5 (confident). Spend 5 minutes on this. You now see exactly which high-weightage topics are weak—these are your biggest score multipliers.

3
Calculate your priority score

Multiply (exam weightage) × (5 minus your current level). Topics with the highest scores are your immediate focus. The lowest-scoring topics go to the bottom of your list, even if they seem important. This single exercise redirects your study time toward maximum impact.

After this: You'll identify 2-4 topics that are simultaneously high-weightage AND where you have knowledge gaps. These topics alone likely represent 30-50% of exam marks. Focusing here first will show measurable score improvement within 2-3 weeks of structured study.
