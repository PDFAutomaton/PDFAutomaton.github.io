You get stuck in research loops, second-guess your choices, or freeze when facing the actual complexity of building, buying, or moving. The problem isn't your commitment—it's that you don't have a system.

This guide gives you the exact framework Stan Wilkins uses to move from decision to execution. You'll get a methodical pathway that handles the three entry points (building, buying, or renting into tiny living), shows you how to process emotional attachment to possessions without shame, and builds your confidence through small, verifiable actions. Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you commit to anything, you need proof that this path is real for you. This quick exercise clarifies your actual entry point (build, buy, or move in), exposes the real obstacles hiding beneath 'I'm not ready yet,' and gives you one concrete action you can take today. This isn't motivation—it's diagnosis.

1
Define your entry point

Write down which path appeals to you most: building a tiny home, buying an existing one, or renting into tiny living. Don't overthink it. If you're genuinely torn between two, write both. This isn't a forever choice—it's your starting assumption.

2
List your three biggest fears about this path

Write them down exactly as you think them. 'What if I build it wrong?' 'What if I can't afford it?' 'What if I move in and hate it?' Don't solve them yet. Just name them. These fears are data—they'll show you where the system needs to catch you.

3
Identify one person who's already done your chosen path

Find one real example: a builder, a buyer, a renter. A YouTube creator, a friend of a friend, a Reddit post. Write down one specific thing they did that proved it was possible. This breaks the abstraction—you're no longer thinking about 'tiny homes in general' but about 'how this actual person moved from zero to done.'

After this: You'll know your entry point, you'll have named the fears that actually matter (not the vague ones), and you'll have proof that someone like you has already done this. You're not further along the path yet—but you're no longer stuck in the fog. That clarity is where execution begins.
