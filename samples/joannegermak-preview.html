The Simple Trading System

Entry Rules That Prevent Losses and Build Consistency

You see the setup. Price is moving, the chart looks right, Twitter is exploding with calls, and you buy in — only to watch it reverse the second you enter. You sell at a loss, it bounces back, and you sit there wondering if you just got unlucky or if you genuinely have no idea what you're doing. Here's the honest answer: it's not bad luck. You don't have a system. You have a reaction habit. And a reaction habit will drain a $2,000 account just as fast as it drains a $200 one.

This guide gives you a rules-based entry system — one that tells you exactly when to get in, when to stay out, and how to size a trade so that a loss doesn't wreck your week. It won't make every trade a winner. Nothing does. What it will do is stop the random, impulsive entries that are quietly bleeding your account, and replace them with a repeatable process you can run on any market, any day, in about 20 minutes. Small wins, compounded over time, beat one big moonshot that wipes you out. That's the whole game.

⚡ Quick Win — Included Free
Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read another chapter, do this exercise right now. It won't make you money today, but it will show you exactly why your last three losing trades happened — and that's worth more than any entry signal you'll find on social media. You're going to build a dead-simple trade decision checklist and immediately test it against a trade you've already taken.

1
Pull up the last trade you lost money on

Open your exchange or brokerage history and find a losing trade from the past 30 days. Screenshot it or write down the asset, your entry price, exit price, and the approximate time of day. Then answer three questions in a notes app or on paper: What made you enter that trade? Were you following a predetermined rule, or did something — a tweet, a price spike, a gut feeling — push you in? And did you decide your exit before you entered, or after? Be brutal. This isn't about guilt, it's about data.

2
Score that trade against a 3-point pre-entry filter

Apply this filter to the trade you just pulled up. Point one: trend alignment — was the trade going in the same direction as the broader trend on a higher timeframe, or were you fighting it? Point two: defined risk — did you know your exact stop-loss level before you entered, or did you just hope it wouldn't drop too far? Point three: reward ratio — was your potential gain at least twice your potential loss, or were you risking $200 to make $80? Score it honestly: zero, one, two, or three points. Most first-time traders score a one. That one point is usually why the trade looked good enough to take.

3
Write your personal entry rule in one sentence

Based on what you just found, write a single rule you will follow before entering any trade this week. It should cover all three filter points in plain language. For example: I will only enter a trade when price is above the 50-day moving average, I have a stop-loss set before I click buy, and my target is at least twice my stop distance. It doesn't have to be sophisticated. It has to be specific enough that you can answer yes or no to it in under 60 seconds. Save it somewhere you will actually see it — your phone lock screen, a sticky note on your monitor, the top of a notes file you open before every session.

✅ Expected result: After 30 minutes you will have one documented losing trade analyzed against a real filter, and one written rule that did not exist before today. That rule is the beginning of your system. Every professional trader you've ever heard of started with a version of this exact exercise — not a perfect algorithm, just a written rule they committed to testing consistently. The rest of this guide builds on top of it.

Stop Gambling, Start Deciding: The Mental Reset That Separates Traders from Losers

You'll learn to recognize the exact difference between a trade backed by a rule and a trade backed by a feeling — so every position you open from this point forward has a reason that exists before you touch your phone.

Right now, 90% of your losses aren't from bad luck — they're from entering trades based on vibes: a YouTube comment, a Reddit thread blowing up, or a chart that 'looks like it's about to go' with zero defined criteria for what that actually means. The foundation of this entire system is one non-negotiable rule: you write down WHY you're entering before you enter, and if you can't write it down in one clear sentence, you don't trade it — full stop, no exceptions. This isn't about being boring or missing moves; it's about recognizing that the market doesn't reward people who react fastest, it rewards people who have already decided what they're looking for before the opportunity shows up. Think of it like working a job with a probation period — you don't get to freestyle your first week, you follow the process until the process becomes instinct, and then you earn the right to adapt it.

  • Open your Notes app right now and create a note called 'Trade Log' — before your next trade, write one sentence in this format: 'I am buying/selling [asset] because [specific observable condition], and I will exit if [specific condition changes]'
  • Scroll back through your last 5 trades or paper trades and honestly label each one as either R (rule-based) or F (feeling-based) — this audit will show you exactly where your money is going and why
  • For the next 7 days, set a rule that you will not open any position in the first 30 minutes of your trading session — use that time to observe only, which forces your brain to shift from reaction mode into analysis mode
💡 Pro tip: The traders who blow accounts fastest are almost always the ones with the highest conviction — certainty feels like an edge but it's actually a blindspot, so your system should be built to survive your own worst impulses, not just market volatility.
