Introduction

You've watched 47 YouTube videos on productivity. Read 12 blog posts about habit stacking. Bookmarked that one tweet thread about deep work. And somehow you're *less* sure what to actually do than when you started.

This guide gives you a dead simple ranking framework—the same psychology that makes tier lists so satisfying to watch—that turns your personal development consumption into actual decisions. No more analysis paralysis. No more 'but what if I'm missing the *real* answer.' Just a clear S-to-F tier system for sorting through the noise and picking what to implement this week.

This guide was created by researching how people actually make decisions when overwhelmed by choice, and what makes ranking systems so effective at cutting through decision fatigue. It's built on observation, not theory.

Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Don't read the whole thing yet. Grab three pieces of advice you've been sitting on and tier them right now. This teaches your brain how the framework works and forces you to make an actual decision instead of collecting more information.

1
Write down three advice pieces you've consumed but never implemented

Could be a productivity hack, a fitness tip, a learning method—anything from a video, article, or podcast you actually watched/read. Be specific. 'Exercise more' doesn't count; 'do 10 minutes of strength training before work' does.

2
Rank them S-tier, A-tier, or B-tier based on one question only

Ask yourself: 'If I could only do ONE of these this week, which would actually change something?' Don't overthink it. Your gut answer is the right one. S-tier = do it immediately. A-tier = do it next. B-tier = maybe someday.

3
Set a 10-minute timer and do the S-tier thing today

Not tomorrow. Not Monday. Today. Even if it's imperfect. The goal is to prove to yourself that you can actually decide and execute, not collect more information.

After this: You'll realize that your paralysis wasn't about needing more advice—it was about needing permission to rank things and pick one. You'll also have one small win under your belt that proves the framework works.
