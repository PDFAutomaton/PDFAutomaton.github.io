Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read further, apply this compressed version of the framework to any current tech problem you're facing. This isn't the full method—it's proof that the approach works. Start with Bismillah and clear intention (niyyah) to solve this deliberately rather than frantically. You'll see how systematic thinking replaces scattered searching.

1
Describe the problem in one sentence without emotion or assumption

Don't say 'my computer is broken' or 'the app keeps crashing.' Say exactly what happens: 'When I open this app on my phone, it shows a spinning circle for 30 seconds then closes' or 'My laptop won't connect to WiFi even though it's turned on.' Write it down. This forces you to observe rather than assume.

2
Test two simple conditions without changing anything

First: Does the problem happen every time or only sometimes? Second: Does it happen only with this app/device/connection, or does it happen everywhere? For example, if an app crashes, test if it crashes when you restart your phone. If WiFi won't connect, test if it connects on a different device using the same WiFi. You're gathering facts, not guessing.

3
Based on your findings, place the problem into one of three categories

Write down which fits: (A) It's a software/app issue—only happens in one place, or only after an update. (B) It's a connection/system issue—happens across multiple apps or devices. (C) It's a hardware issue—the device itself isn't responding. This takes 2 minutes but saves you from wasting an hour on the wrong solution.

After this: You now know your problem's category and whether it's consistent or random. You've eliminated half the possible causes without downloading anything, restarting randomly, or taking wild guesses. This is the difference between troubleshooting and problem-solving.
