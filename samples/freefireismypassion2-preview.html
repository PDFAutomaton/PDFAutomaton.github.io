⚡ Quick Win — Included Free
Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read another page of theory, do THIS. This is the Single Gun Drift Test — a fast diagnostic that tells you immediately whether your current sensitivity is too high, too low, or in the right range for your most-used gun. Most players have never done this properly. You will finish this in 30 minutes and walk away knowing something real and actionable about your current settings TODAY.

1
Pick ONE gun and go into Training Mode right now

Open Free Fire, go to Training Mode, and select only the gun you use most in ranked — whether that's MP40, M1887, AK, SCAR, or anything else. Equip NO attachments except the scope or sight you actually use in real matches. Stand exactly 20 meters from a dummy target (count the floor tiles if needed — each large tile is roughly 1 meter). This distance matters because it is the most common close-to-mid range where most ranked gunfights are decided. Do not use a bot lobby, do not use a real match — Training Mode only, because you need zero pressure to observe honestly.

2
Fire a full magazine and record where your shots land on the dummy

ADS into the dummy's chest and fire the full magazine without consciously correcting your aim after the first shot. Just fire and let the recoil do whatever it does naturally. After the magazine is empty, look at the hit pattern on the dummy. Now categorize what you see: if all your shots drifted UP past the head and you lost the target completely, your ADS sensitivity is too HIGH for this gun. If your shots stayed glued to the chest and barely moved, your sensitivity is too LOW and you are overcompensating by gripping too hard. If shots scattered LEFT or RIGHT in a wide arc, your horizontal sensitivity is mismatched. Screenshot or mentally note the pattern — this is your current aim signature and it is specific to you and your device.

3
Make ONE single adjustment based on what you saw and test again immediately

Based on your pattern, go to Settings and change ONLY your ADS sensitivity for that specific gun category — move it by 3 points MAXIMUM in the direction that fixes your observed drift. If shots went too high, reduce by 3. If too stiff and low, increase by 3. Then repeat the exact same test: same gun, same distance, same full magazine, no correction. Compare the new pattern to the old one. You are NOT looking for perfection right now — you are looking for DIRECTION. Did the pattern move toward center? That tells you which way to keep adjusting in the full system. Write down both numbers and the result. This is the beginning of your personal sensitivity log and it is more valuable than any number you will ever copy from YouTube.

✅ Expected result: After completing this test you will know two things you did not know 30 minutes ago: which direction your current sensitivity is drifting for your main gun, and whether your problem is a settings issue or a muscle memory issue. If the adjusted setting immediately felt more controlled, your original sensitivity was miscalibrated for your device — and the full guide will show you how to dial every gun and distance into the same kind of precision. If even the adjusted setting felt inconsistent, your foundation is actually a mechanical training issue — and the guide covers that diagnostic path too. Either way, you now have a real data point about YOUR aim instead of someone else's opinion about theirs.
