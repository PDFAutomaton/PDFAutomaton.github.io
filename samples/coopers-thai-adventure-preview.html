Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read another word, you need to know your starting position. This quick framework will show you whether you're in the 'Thailand is probably viable' camp or the 'I need to be really careful here' camp. It's not a judgment—it's clarity. Think of this like doing your due diligence before any major commitment. The Prophet (PBUH) emphasized seeking knowledge (ilm) before making decisions, and that's exactly what this is.

1
Map your three financial numbers

Write down: (1) Your guaranteed monthly income in retirement (pensions, investments, rental income—things you can count on). (2) Your current monthly expenses at home. (3) Your target monthly budget in Thailand. Don't guess. Use last year's bank statements. This takes 10 minutes and gives you your financial baseline.

2
Rate your health and healthcare comfort on three dimensions

On a scale of 1–10, rate: (1) Your current health stability (are you managing chronic conditions well or are they unpredictable?). (2) Your comfort level with using foreign healthcare (have you done it before? Does it stress you out?). (3) Your ability to access English-speaking medical care if needed. Write one sentence for each. This reveals if healthcare logistics will be a hidden cost or a non-issue for you.

3
Identify your social anchor—the one thing that will keep you connected

Thailand is wonderful, but it's not home. What will keep you grounded? Will it be a community (mosque, hobby group, expat friends, work you're passionate about)? Family connections (video calls, visits)? A purpose or project? Write one thing. This isn't about avoiding loneliness—it's about having intentional commitment to something that matters to you, which in Islam we'd call an amanah (a responsibility you're taking on).

After this: After 30 minutes you'll have: Your financial gap (how much you actually need vs. what you have), a realistic assessment of your healthcare situation, and clarity on what's going to anchor you during transitions. You'll know whether to read this guide as 'confirming my plan' or 'helping me navigate real challenges.' Either way, you're moving forward with intention instead of hope.
