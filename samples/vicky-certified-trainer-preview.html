Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you begin the full 30-day reset, you're going to experience what consistency actually feels like. This quick assessment does two things: it shows you where your baseline strength is (so you can genuinely see progress), and it reminds your body what it's capable of. Think of this as setting your niyyah—your intention—and letting your body feel the difference immediately. There's barakah in knowing exactly where you're starting from.

1
Find three everyday movements you already do

Stand up from a chair 10 times, slowly. Carry your heaviest grocery bag or laundry basket from one room to another. Hold a plank position (on your knees is fine) for as long as feels manageable. Don't strain—just notice. Write down how each feels and how long you lasted.

2
Do this exact same thing today—but just three times through the week

Monday, Wednesday, Friday. Same three movements. Same tracking. Nothing fancy. This is your baseline, and it's also your first taste of what 'showing up' actually means. You're not trying to improve yet. You're just practicing the skill of noticing.

3
Write down one specific moment from your day where strength would change everything

Maybe it's carrying your child up the stairs without getting winded. Maybe it's opening a jar without asking for help. Maybe it's standing through a full day of work without back pain. Keep this visible. This is your 'why'—and in Islam, intention (niyyah) is what transforms effort into ibadah, into something that matters beyond the physical.

After this: By the end of this week, you'll notice one small difference—maybe the chair-to-stand feels slightly less wobbly, or you carried the basket without thinking twice. More importantly, you'll have practiced showing up three times. That's not motivation. That's the beginning of a skill. You'll have real baseline numbers to compare against in week four. And you'll have a clear reason why this matters to you specifically—not why it matters in general, but why it matters to *you*.
