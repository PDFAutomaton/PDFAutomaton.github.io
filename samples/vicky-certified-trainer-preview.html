It targets one of the most common and most damaging pronunciation errors for South Asian English speakers in professional settings — the mispronunciation of words ending in 'tion' combined with incorrect word stress. This single error appears in words like 'pronunciation,' 'communication,' 'presentation,' and 'qualification' — exactly the words you use most in interviews and exams. Fixing even the stress pattern on these words today will produce an immediately audible difference. Set your intention, say Bismillah, and give yourself 30 focused minutes. If you are between Fajr and Zuhr, this is an excellent use of that early barakah in your time.

1
Record yourself saying these five words naturally, exactly as you say them right now — do not try to correct anything yet

Open the voice memo app on your phone. Say each of these words once, naturally, at normal speaking speed: pronunciation, communication, presentation, qualification, organization. Do not look up how to say them first. Do not slow down. Say them the way you actually say them when you are speaking in real life. Save the recording. This is your before sample and it is important. Most learners skip this step. Do not skip it. You cannot measure improvement without a baseline, and you will want to hear the difference in 20 minutes when you record again.

2
Learn and drill the correct stress pattern for all five words using the syllable-tapping method

Here is the rule these five words share: the stress in 'tion' words almost always falls on the syllable directly before 'tion.' So the pattern is: pro-nun-ci-A-tion, com-mu-ni-CA-tion, pre-sen-TA-tion, qual-i-fi-CA-tion, or-gan-i-ZA-tion. The syllable in capitals gets the weight — it is slightly louder, slightly longer, and slightly higher in pitch than the others. Now tap your finger on the table for each syllable and hit the table harder on the stressed syllable. Do this physically, not just mentally. Say each word ten times while tapping. pro-nun-ci-A-tion. tap tap tap TAP tap. Your mouth needs muscle memory, not just understanding. Also correct the 'tion' ending itself — it sounds like 'shun,' not 'see-on' and not 'ti-on.' The ending is always a soft, quick 'shun.' Run through all five words ten times each with the tapping method. This takes roughly 12 to 15 minutes if you do it properly.

3
Record yourself saying the same five words again and compare the two recordings back to back

Open your voice memo app again. Say the same five words in the same order at normal speaking speed. Save it. Now play your first recording and your second recording back to back. Listen specifically for two things: one, whether the stress is landing on the correct syllable now, and two, whether the ending sounds like a clean 'shun' rather than a drawn-out or broken ending. Most people hear a clear difference on at least three of the five words after a single focused drill session. If you hear the difference, that is not a small thing — that is your ear and your mouth beginning to recalibrate. Note which words still sound off. Those become your personal priority list for Day One of the full system.

You will also have identified your personal weak points within this word category — which is more useful than any general advice because it means your practice tomorrow is already targeted. More importantly, you will have broken the cycle of passive learning. You did not watch a video. You corrected something.
