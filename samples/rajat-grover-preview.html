Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read anything else, do this one exercise right now. Most people spend weeks in research paralysis because they're trying to plan everything at once. This exercise collapses your uncertainty down to a single, accurate daily cost number for one destination in under 30 minutes. Once you have that number, the rest of the planning becomes simple arithmetic. You need a phone, a notepad or notes app, and access to Booking.com and Google Maps.

1
Pick one destination and pull up Booking.com for your actual travel dates

Choose Bangkok, Bali, or Kuala Lumpur — whichever is on your shortlist. Open Booking.com, enter your exact travel dates, filter by Guest Rating 7+ and price range ₹1,500 to ₹2,800 per night, and look at the first five results in areas Sukhumvit Soi 11 area for Bangkok, Seminyak or Kuta for Bali, Bukit Bintang for KL. Write down the actual nightly price of the third result — not the cheapest, not the most expensive. This is your realistic accommodation baseline. A standard private room in these areas in these price brackets looks like: Bangkok November ₹1,900 per night at places like Nap Krathom or similar guesthouses, Bali November ₹2,200 per night at mid-range private villas or clean hotels in Kuta, KL November ₹1,600 per night at hotels in the Bukit Bintang corridor. Write that number down. That is your accommodation cost per day.

2
Calculate your food cost using the 3-meal formula for that destination

This is the formula: Street meal or food court lunch plus sit-down dinner plus breakfast equals your daily food number. Use these verified price anchors from 2024. Bangkok: breakfast at a 7-Eleven or street stall ₹120 to ₹180, lunch at a food court like MBK or terminal 21 food court ₹250 to ₹380, dinner at a mid-range Thai restaurant ₹500 to ₹700, total daily food ₹870 to ₹1,260, use ₹1,100 as your working number. Bali: breakfast at a warung ₹200 to ₹300, lunch at a local nasi goreng spot ₹300 to ₹450, dinner at a mid-level restaurant in Seminyak ₹600 to ₹900, total ₹1,100 to ₹1,650, use ₹1,350. KL: breakfast at a mamak stall ₹150 to ₹200, lunch at a hawker center ₹280 to ₹380, dinner at a mid-range restaurant ₹450 to ₹650, total ₹880 to ₹1,230, use ₹1,000. Write down your destination's food number.

3
Add transport and one activity to get your complete daily cost number

Local transport daily average by destination: Bangkok BTS Skytrain plus one Grab ride equals roughly ₹400 to ₹600 per day, use ₹500. Bali scooter rental ₹350 to ₹500 per day plus petrol ₹80, use ₹500. KL MRT plus one Grab equals ₹250 to ₹400, use ₹320. For activities divide your total planned activity spend by your trip duration. Example: Bangkok temples are free, Muay Thai ticket ₹1,500 to ₹2,500 for a good stadium seat, cooking class ₹2,800 to ₹3,500 for a half-day class, island day trip ₹2,500 to ₹4,000. If you plan three paid activities across 7 days that totals roughly ₹8,000 divided by 7 equals ₹1,140 per day. Now add your three numbers: accommodation plus food plus transport plus daily activity average. A Bangkok 7-day trip example: ₹1,900 plus ₹1,100 plus ₹500 plus ₹1,140 equals ₹4,640 per day, times 7 days equals ₹32,480 on the ground, add flights and visa and you are looking at a realistic ₹52,000 to ₹58,000 total. Write your number down. That is your baseline. The rest of this guide will show you exactly how to compress it, stretch it, or validate it against month-by-month pricing data.

✅ Expected result: In 30 minutes you now have a single daily cost number and a rough total trip cost for your destination. It will not be perfect yet — that is what the rest of the guide is for — but it will be within 15 percent of your actual spend, which is already more accurate than anything you will get from three hours of Reddit browsing. The ₹ Reality Check: Why Your Southeast Asia Budget Is Wrong Before You Even Start

Shift from vague excitement to a concrete cost-per-day mental model so you never again confuse 'cheap destination' with 'I'll figure it out later.'

Most guys from Nagpur or Indore planning their first Bangkok or Bali trip make the same mistake Rajat sees constantly in food travel — they budget for the dish price but forget the full meal cost: flights, visa, SIM, travel insurance, airport transfers, and the inevitable 'just one more street food stop' all stack up fast. The real framework is thinking in three buckets: Fixed Costs (flights + visa + insurance, non-negotiable, book these first), Daily Burn Rate (accommodation + food + local transport as a single per-day number, not separate), and Buffer Money (minimum 15% of total, not optional, because ATM fees, upset stomachs, and missed buses are not hypotheticals in Southeast Asia). For reference, a realistic Daily Burn Rate in Thailand is ₹2,800–3,500/day if you eat local and stay in decent hostels or budget hotels — not the ₹800/day you saw in that 2019 YouTube video — and Vietnam runs ₹2,200–2,800/day right now post-inflation. Once you lock these three buckets into a single number before opening any booking site, you stop researching and start deciding, which is what kills 80% of trips in the planning phase.

  • Open a Google Sheet right now and create three columns — Fixed Costs, Daily Burn Rate, Buffer — then drop in rough numbers for one destination you're actually considering (use ₹35,000–45,000 for a return flight to Bangkok from Delhi/Mumbai as your starting anchor)
  • Go to Rajat's food travel content and pick any Southeast Asia video — pause it every time a food item appears and note the price shown, then multiply by 5 to estimate a realistic daily food budget including drinks, snacks, and one sit-down meal
  • Set a 'Trip Decision Deadline' — pick a date exactly 7 days from today and write it in your phone calendar as 'SEA Trip: Go or No-Go' so this planning session has a forcing function instead of becoming another saved tab
💡 Pro tip: Your biggest hidden cost isn't food or accommodation — it's the 3–4% forex markup on every card transaction plus the ₹200–500 ATM fee per withdrawal, which can quietly add ₹3,000–5,000 to a 10-day trip; apply for a Niyo Global or IndusInd Indulge card before you book anything else, because this single step costs you nothing and saves more than a night's accommodation.
