Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read further, run this quick test. It takes 30 minutes and will show you exactly where your focus breaks down. This is your baseline. You'll return to this in 30 days and see the difference.

1
Pick one task that matters

Not email, not admin work. Something that actually moves a project forward. Something you've been thinking about starting but haven't. Set a timer for 25 minutes.

2
Work until the timer stops

Write down the exact time you first felt the urge to switch. Don't judge it. Just note it—when did your mind wander? When did you reach for your phone? This is your distraction point.

3
Document what you finished

Write down what you actually completed in those 25 minutes. Be specific. One page written. Three paragraphs edited. One sketch finished. This is your current output baseline.

After this: You now have three pieces of data: your distraction point, your output per 25 minutes, and proof that you can sustain at least some focus. In 30 days, you'll repeat this same task and your distraction point will be gone. Your output will double.
