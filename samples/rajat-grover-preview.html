Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read another page, let's prove this works. In 30 minutes, you'll create the backbone of your entire money system—and probably find money you didn't know you had.

1
Open a simple notebook or Google Doc titled 'Monthly Money Dashboard'

Create three sections: Income (what came in), Fixed Expenses (rent, insurance, subscriptions), and Variable Expenses (everything else). Don't overthink categories yet. You're just capturing the shape of your money.

2
Pull your last 3 months of bank statements and list every transaction you've made

Spend 10 minutes scanning. You're not analyzing—you're just getting it out of your head and onto paper. This clears mental clutter instantly.

3
Identify one tax-deductible expense you're currently paying (NPS, health insurance, rent, business tools, education)

Just one. Write it down with the annual amount. You'll formalize this in the system, but for now, you've spotted at least one area where you can reduce your tax burden.

After this: You now have clarity on where your money actually goes (not where you think it goes), you've reduced mental friction by externalizing your finances, and you've identified at least one deduction you were probably overlooking. That's the foundation. Everything else builds from here.
