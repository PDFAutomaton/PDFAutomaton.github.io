Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read another word, let's prove this works. In the next 30 minutes, you'll find at least ₹5,000-15,000 you're currently losing to poor tracking or overpayment. This is your confidence builder. Your 'oh, this actually works' moment.

1
Pull Your Last 3 Months of Bank & Credit Card Statements

Open your bank app. Download statements from January-March (or last 3 months if you're reading this mid-year). Do the same for credit cards. Paste them into a blank Google Sheet. Don't organize yet—just get all transaction data in one place. Time: 5 minutes.

2
Identify Your 'Tax-Deductible But Forgotten' Expenses

Scan for: medical bills (doctor visits, lab tests, medicines), professional development (online courses, books, certifications), home office setup (laptop, desk, chair), health insurance premiums, donations to NGOs. Mark these with a yellow highlight. Most people forget ₹40,000-80,000 of valid deductions every year just because they never looked. Example: You spent ₹8,000 on a course in February—that's deductible under Section 80D or business expense. Write down your total forgotten deductions. Time: 10 minutes.

3
Calculate Your 'Tax Leakage'

For every deductible expense you found, multiply by your tax bracket. If you're in 20% bracket and found ₹50,000 in deductions you forgot to track—that's ₹10,000 you're overpaying in tax. If you found ₹80,000 in deductions, that's ₹16,000. Write down your number. This is what you recover in Year 1 just by having a system. Time: 5 minutes.

After this: Most people completing this find ₹5,000-20,000 in forgotten deductions. That's ₹1,000-4,000 in immediate tax savings. But more importantly—you've just proven that you have a tracking problem, not an income problem. This guide fixes the tracking problem. Everything else flows from here.
