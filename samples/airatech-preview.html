Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read further, you're going to complete one concrete task. Not to feel productive, but because clarity compounds. When you know your exact gap—the distance between where you are and where you want to be—the rest becomes inevitable. This is how discipline works in Islam: we set intention (niyyah), we identify what separates us from that goal, and we act. This single step shifts you from dreaming to doing.

1
Calculate your true monthly expenses

Write down every expense from last month—fixed costs (rent, utilities, subscriptions) and variable costs (food, transport, entertainment). Be honest. This number is sacred information. It's the foundation of everything that follows. When you know this figure, you remove the vagueness that keeps people stuck.

2
Define your destination and trip length

Pick one specific place you want to visit in the next 6 months and decide how long you'll stay (7-14 days is ideal for a first solo trip). Write it down. Not someday. Not eventually. A real destination. A real timeframe. This becomes your north star—everything you do for the next six months points toward this.

3
Calculate the total cost of that trip

Research flights, accommodation, daily expenses (food, transport, activities) for your chosen destination and duration. Add 20% buffer. This is your target number. Subtract it from your current savings. What remains is your gap—the amount you need to bridge in 6 months. Write this number down too. It's no longer abstract.

After this: You now have three numbers: your monthly expenses, your trip cost, and your savings gap. You've moved from 'I want to travel' to 'I need to save $X by [date].' This clarity alone shifts something. The fantasy becomes a project. And projects have solutions.
