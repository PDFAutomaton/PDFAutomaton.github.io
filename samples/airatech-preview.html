Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before anything else, you need clarity on what's actually possible within your real constraints. This 30-minute exercise removes the guesswork and gives you a concrete number: how many trips you can genuinely afford and take this year, without overextending.

1
Map your actual leave balance

Pull up your HR portal or ask your manager: how many days of leave do you have? How many are already committed (family visits, weddings, known obligations)? Subtract these. This is your real travel leave pool. Write this number down.

2
Calculate your discretionary travel budget

Take your monthly surplus (income minus essential expenses, no fantasy budget). Multiply by 12, then subtract one month's buffer. Now divide by 4 (to account for seasons and irregular expenses). This is your per-trip budget. Round down. This is the number that matters.

3
Decide your trip mix

You have a leave pool and a budget pool. Now decide: will you take 3 shorter trips (4-5 days) or 2 longer trips (7-10 days) this year? Or a mix? This single decision removes 80% of future confusion.

After this: You now have three written numbers: your available leave days, your per-trip budget, and your trip structure for the year. This is not aspirational. This is what's actually possible. Everything that follows works within these numbers.
