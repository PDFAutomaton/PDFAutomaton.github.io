Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you dive into the full system, try this single protocol right now. It takes 30 minutes, uses material you're already studying, and will show you immediately why this approach works. You'll finish this session remembering more than you typically do from an hour of reading.

1
Choose one short study session (10-15 minutes of reading)

Pick a chapter section, article, or concept you need to learn. Something you'd normally read passively. Keep it short—this is a test run, not your full study session.

2
Read with a specific question in your mind

Before you start, write down one question you want answered by the end of reading: 'What is the main concept here?' or 'How does this apply to what I learned last week?' Read only to answer that question. Your brain will naturally focus when it has a target.

3
Stop and explain it in your own words (2 minutes)

After reading, close the book. Spend 2 minutes explaining what you just read to an imaginary person who knows nothing about the topic. Write it down or say it aloud. This forces your brain to actually process the material, not just move your eyes over it.

After this: You'll finish those 30 minutes with a clear understanding of what you read and a written summary you can review later. Compare this to how you usually feel after reading—the difference will be obvious. This is what the full system builds on.
