Introduction

You have ideas. Real, beautiful ideas. But somewhere between that spark and actually publishing, everything falls apart. One week you're posting daily. The next week, nothing. You're not lazy—you're just trying to figure out how to make it all work without losing yourself in the process.

This guide gives you a real system for maintaining a consistent publishing schedule that doesn't require you to be glued to your phone or spend hours on technical setup. By the end, you'll have a framework you can start using today.

This guide was created through conversations with content creators, researchers studying creator workflows, and people who've built sustainable creative practices. It's written with genuine respect for the work you're doing and the real constraints you face—not theories, but practical steps that actually work.

Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before we dive deeper, let's create something concrete. In the next 30 minutes, you're going to map out one week of content and see immediately how much clearer things become.

1
Open a simple document or notebook

Google Doc, Notion, pen and paper—whatever feels natural. You're not building a complex system yet. This is just for you.

2
List 7 content ideas (one for each day this week)

Don't overthink it. These can be short: 'morning routine thoughts,' 'what I learned today,' 'a question I've been asked.' Real, simple ideas.

3
Assign each idea to a day and a time

Pick one day for each idea. Pick one realistic time you can create (not 6am if you hate mornings). That's it.

After this: You'll have a visible, doable plan for one week. This clarity alone will reduce the 'what do I post?' anxiety by at least 50%. Publish one piece from this plan today. You've already started.
