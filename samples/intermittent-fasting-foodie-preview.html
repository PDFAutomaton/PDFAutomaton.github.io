Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read further, complete this quick exercise. It takes 30 minutes and will immediately show you where your current system is breaking down—and what to fix first. This isn't theory. This is data about your actual life.

1
Inventory your past attempts (15 minutes)

Write down every diet, fasting protocol, or fitness plan you've started in the last 3-5 years. For each one, note: What week did you stop? What happened that week? What did you need that you didn't have? (Examples: clearer rules, flexibility, accountability, visible results, better timing, support, a protocol for 'messy days'). Don't judge yourself. Just observe the pattern. You'll likely see the same breaking point repeating.

2
Define your ONE biggest friction point (10 minutes)

From your inventory, circle the most common reason you stopped. Is it afternoon hunger? Skipped meals affecting your energy for Asr or Maghrib prayers? Family dinners disrupting your window? Feeling deprived? Seeing no results in the first two weeks? Not having a backup plan for busy days? Identify the one friction point that shows up across multiple attempts. This is what the 90-day system will solve first.

3
Set your intention (niyyah) with Bismillah (5 minutes)

Before reading the rest of this guide, take a breath. Say Bismillah. Write down your intention for these 90 days in one sentence. Not 'lose 20 pounds' but something deeper: 'I'm building discipline (sabr) as an act of worship and caring for the body Allah gave me.' This isn't motivation. It's your anchor when willpower dips. Intention transforms a diet into an amanah—a responsibility you've accepted before Allah.

After this: You now have clarity on exactly where your system breaks, exactly what triggered it, and a reason deeper than a number on the scale. This is your starting point—not shame, just information. Most women find that their 'quitting point' is actually the same friction point across every attempt. Once you see it, the 90-day system shows you how to design around it instead of fighting it.
