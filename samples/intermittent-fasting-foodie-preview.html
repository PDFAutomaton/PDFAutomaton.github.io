Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you even start fasting, you need a realistic eating window that fits your actual life. This exercise shows you where your natural rhythm already is, then you'll anchor your fasting window to it. Most people skip this step and wonder why their system falls apart.

1
Map your non-negotiable eating moments

Grab a piece of paper or your phone notes. Write down the three meals or snacks you absolutely cannot move or skip this week—the ones tied to work, family, or just survival. (Example: coffee at 7 AM because you can't function, lunch at 12:30 because it's a standing meeting, dinner around 7 PM with your family.) Don't overthink it. Just write what's already locked in.

2
Identify the largest natural gap in your eating

Look at those locked-in times. What's the longest stretch between them where you're not already eating or expected to eat? That's your fasting window waiting to happen. If you eat at 7 AM and don't eat again until noon, boom—you've got a natural 5-hour fast already happening. If your dinner's at 7 PM and breakfast at 8 AM, that's already 13 hours. These gaps are where IF lives, not against them.

3
Build your first week's eating window around what's already working

Now set a simple eating window that honors those locked-in moments. If coffee at 7 AM and dinner at 7 PM bookend your day, your eating window might be 7 AM to 7 PM, which means fasting from 7 PM to 7 AM (12 hours—totally sustainable). Or if you prefer skipping breakfast because mornings are rushed anyway, shift it to noon-8 PM. The point: your window should feel like relief, not restriction.

After this: You'll have identified your natural eating rhythm and created a fasting window that doesn't fight your actual life. Most people realize they're already fasting accidentally during parts of their day—they just didn't have a name for it. This becomes your foundation. You're not starting from zero. You're starting from what's already working.
