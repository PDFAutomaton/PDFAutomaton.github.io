Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before we go deeper, let's evaluate one tech purchase you're considering right now. This quick exercise will show you how the framework works in real life.

1
Write down the gadget or device you're thinking about buying

Be specific. Not just 'a laptop'—the exact model, brand, and price. Example: 'MacBook Air M2, ₹89,999' or 'Samsung Galaxy S23, ₹69,999'. Put it at the top of a sheet of paper or a notes app.

2
Write down your current problem it would solve

What's broken or missing right now? Be honest. 'My current laptop is slow when I edit videos' is real. 'Everyone else has a newer phone' is not a problem it will solve. Write one or two concrete pain points.

3
Ask yourself: Will this specific device actually solve this specific problem better than what I have now?

Not 'might it be nice to have.' Will it materially improve the situation? If you're editing videos and your current laptop takes 2 hours to export, will this new one cut it to 45 minutes? Then yes—there's real value. If you're buying it because 'it's newer'—you already know the answer.

After this: You'll immediately see whether this purchase is driven by a real need or by FOMO and marketing. Most people find that 60-70% of their planned tech purchases don't pass this basic test. That's not failure—that's you saving money before you waste it.
