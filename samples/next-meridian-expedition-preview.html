Introduction

You've imagined the departure a thousand times. The problem isn't the dream—it's the gap between wanting to go and knowing exactly how to get there. Most people stall in that gap, not because they lack courage, but because they lack a structure.

This guide gives you that structure. A decision framework so clear and modular that you can move from 'someday' to 'departure date confirmed' without the financial shock, logistical paralysis, or decision fatigue that stops most people. You'll break a six-month project into three 30-day phases, each with non-negotiable checkpoints and win criteria.

This guide was developed through research into how remote workers, sabbatical planners, and lifestyle-shift seekers actually execute major life transitions. It's built on what works, stripped of what doesn't.

Get Your First Result in 30 Minutes

Before you read further, you'll establish your expedition's core parameters. This isn't brainstorming. This is constraint mapping—the difference between a dream and a project with dates.

1
Define Your Hard Stop Date

Choose a departure date 90 days from today. Write it down. This isn't aspirational—it's your first boundary. Everything else locks into this anchor point. No flexibility here means all other decisions become simpler.

2
Map Your Three Non-Negotiables

What must be true before you leave? (Savings target. Client transition. Visa status. Housing solution.) List exactly three. These are your load-bearing walls. Everything else is decoration.

3
Identify Your Current Bottleneck

Of those three non-negotiables, which one would stop you if it failed? That's your Phase 1 priority. Write it in one sentence: 'I need to [specific outcome] by [date 30 days from now].'

After this: You now have a departure date that's real, three clear success criteria, and one immediate focus area. You've moved from 'I want to travel' to 'I'm traveling on [date] if [specific thing] gets solved.' That shift is everything.
