The problem

Every new client starts the same way: a scramble.

You win the work, then onboarding happens off the top of someone's head — usually yours. It works, mostly. But it's different every time, and the cracks show.

  • You're the only one who really knows how a new client should be brought on.
  • Every kickoff is improvised — different emails, different steps, different gaps.
  • New clients wait, wonder what's happening, and form their first impression on silence.
  • Your team answers the same setup questions from scratch, every single time.
  • You've meant to "write the onboarding doc" for a year. It never reaches the top of the list.
